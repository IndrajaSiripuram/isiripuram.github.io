OVERVIEW

Edu is a app which provides free courses for learning. This app has a language switching option which helps users understand easily and also it provides meaning for the words which makes it even more easily to learn. It has a wide range of topics and subjects to learn from. If the app doesn’t have any courses in the topic you wanted it provides you with the links of free courses available online so you can click on the link and start learning, which saves the time of the users. This app also has a reminder option and track the learning option.The Users can take a test any time and check their improvement.

The Problem

There are so many apps that provide free courses but its like a trial version for a period of 7-10 days and needs payment after that. Also users wish to download the courses and browse it later even when offline. For students with different language background its difficult to understand few terms in English so they have to keep switching between the dictionary and the app.

The Solution

EDU provides all the courses for free without any limits to learn.This app also has captions available in multiple languages to make it easy for students with different language backgrounds.Also it has a download option where users can download the course they are interested in and learn it without worrying about data usage.

USER RESEARCH SUMMERY

Study type: Unmoderated study

Number of participants: 5

Location: Remote

Duration: 15 min

PAIN POINTS

Budget

The subscription of the apps are expensive and a single app doesn’t have all the courses the user needs.

Time Constraint

After finding a course and realizing that it takes more time than the user expected or planned for.

Internet problem

Most of the students in rural areas can’t access the courses because of the network issues and internet problems.

Language

People with non English background have to search in other app/web for the meanings of the words they dont understand.

PAPER WIREFRAMES

The main goal of the app was to make it easy for the users to access the courses and learn without having to worry about paying for it.

The wireframes were designed by sketching some quick alternatives to each screen and the best options were marked and are put together in a single screen and formed a wireframe.

DIGITAL WIREFRAMES

In order to address most of the users pain points we have added filters in the appIn order to address most of the users pain points we have made the login process simple and provided wide range of topics to choose from and learn. If the app doesn’t have any courses available for the users search then the links with the relevant search is provided from other free websites which on click will redirect to the Original webpage of the course. This avoids the time waste during the search.

LOW-FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

The digital wireframes are modified and low-fi prototypes are designed to make it easy to understand the flow and test.

USABILITY STUDY FINDINGS

We conducted two rounds of unmoderated usability with 5 participants. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups and the second study used the high fidelity prototypes and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 findings

User wants easy navigation without any confusion.

User wants to know the duration of the course before they start.

User expects reminders through Email or in app to keep motivated.

Round 2 findings

User wants the language change option.

User likes to see the progress they have made in the course so far.

Notifications when new courses relavant to search are added.

TAKE AWAYS

In conclusion, the design process for the EDU app involved conducting user research, creating personas, conducting competitive analysis, sketching and prototyping, and conducting usability testing. Through this process, I was able to identify the key pain points and desires of users, and design an app that addresses those needs. The final result is an app that makes it easy for people to learn without thinking of their budget and utilizing all their time learning. I learned a lot throughout the process, also gained valuable insights into the needs and preferences of my target audience, and I improved my design skills and knowledge of user-centered design principles.
